# Share Skills with Others

Since skills and commands are just files in a repo, sharing them is as simple as sharing a link. Others can copy your skills into their repos, fork your entire setup, or you can create a template repo that anyone can use as a starting point.

## How to do it

1. Make sure your repo is **public** (Settings > General > Danger Zone > Change visibility)
2. Share the link to your repo or specific files
3. Others can browse your `.claude/skills/` and `.claude/commands/` folders
4. They copy any files they want into their own repos

To make a template repo:

1. Go to **Settings** > **General**
2. Check **Template repository**
3. Now anyone can click **Use this template** to create their own copy

## Try it

```
Share your GitHub OS repo URL with someone:

https://github.com/YOUR-USERNAME/YOUR-REPO-NAME

They can browse to .claude/skills/ and .claude/commands/ to see your setup.
Or if it's a template, they click "Use this template" to get their own copy with all your skills pre-loaded.
```

## Resources

## Tips

- Template repos are the easiest way to share a full setup. The other person gets everything -- skills, commands, issue templates, folder structure -- without affecting your original repo.
- When sharing individual skills, include the SKILL.md file and a brief explanation of what it does and how to use it.
